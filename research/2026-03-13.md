# Closr Block Research — March 13, 2026
## Focus: Scroll-Driven Storytelling & Reveal Mechanics

Previous research: March 8 (typography/text animations), March 12 (particle systems/canvas). This session explores **scroll-driven and gesture-driven reveal mechanics** — how the act of navigating *through* a surprise becomes part of the emotional experience. The viewer isn't just reading; they're *uncovering*.

Existing blocks reviewed: 20 blocks including `scratch-reveal` (scratch-off mechanic), `story-cinema` (cinematic scenes), `classified-dossier` (redacted text reveal). This research avoids those exact patterns and explores NEW reveal/progression approaches.

---

### 1. Parallax Depth Layers (CSS scroll-driven animations)

**Concept:** The surprise is a single vertical scroll where content lives at different visual "depths." As the viewer scrolls, foreground elements move faster than background elements, creating a 3D diorama effect. Text appears to float between layers of atmospheric imagery — clouds, stars, bokeh lights, or abstract color fields.

**Why it works for Closr:** Transforms a linear message into a spatial *journey*. The depth creates a sense of entering something — diving into the sender's feelings. Each scroll increment reveals more, but the parallax makes it feel like the world is shifting around you, not just text appearing.

**Technical approach:**
- CSS `scroll-timeline` + `animation-timeline: scroll()` (native scroll-driven animations, Chrome/Edge, Safari 16.4+)
- Fallback: IntersectionObserver + CSS custom properties for progress
- 3-4 depth layers: far-bg (0.2x scroll speed), mid-bg (0.5x), content (1x), foreground-accent (1.3x)
- Each layer: a full-viewport `position: sticky` element with `transform: translateY(calc(var(--scroll) * Npx))`
- Content appears via `opacity` + `translateY` keyed to scroll position
- Background layers: CSS gradients transitioning hue as you scroll (night sky → dawn → golden hour)
- Keep total scroll height to ~4-5 viewport heights (not too long on mobile)

**Emotional tone:** Epic, cinematic, immersive
**Best for block types:** Story of Us, Future Dreams, Love Letter, Memory Replay

### 2. Fold/Unfold Paper Reveal

**Concept:** The surprise starts as a folded piece of paper (or envelope). The viewer taps/swipes to unfold it — each fold reveals a new section of content. CSS 3D transforms create realistic paper-folding physics. The paper texture, the slight shadow between folds, the satisfying snap of each section unfolding — it all feels tactile.

**Why it works for Closr:** Evokes the physical intimacy of receiving a handwritten note. The act of unfolding mirrors opening a real letter — there's anticipation in each fold. It slows the viewer down deliberately, making each revealed section feel like a gift.

**Technical approach:**
- CSS `perspective`, `transform-style: preserve-3d`, `rotateX()` for fold animation
- Each fold section: a `div` with front/back faces (like a card flip but along an edge)
- Fold sequence: top-down or center-out, each fold triggered by tap or auto-sequence with delay
- Paper texture: subtle `background-image` with fiber noise (CSS repeating gradient trick, no image file)
- Shadow between folds: `box-shadow` animating from tight/dark to diffuse/light as fold opens
- Crease line: thin `border` or `linear-gradient` where the fold was
- Sound pairing: soft paper-rustle (Web Audio API, short buffer, optional)
- 3-5 fold sections per block (more feels tedious)

**Emotional tone:** Intimate, nostalgic, personal
**Best for block types:** Love Letter, Appreciation, Promise, Comfort

### 3. Fog/Mist Wipe Reveal

**Concept:** The screen starts covered in animated fog/mist. The viewer wipes it away with their finger (touch events on canvas), gradually revealing content underneath — like wiping condensation off a window to see a message written in the steam. The fog slowly creeps back if you stop wiping, creating gentle urgency.

**Why it works for Closr:** The "writing on a foggy window" trope is universally romantic. It's playful and interactive — you literally have to work to see the message, making it more rewarding. The creeping-back fog adds game-like tension without frustration.

**Technical approach:**
- Two layers: content (HTML below), fog (canvas overlay)
- Fog canvas: fill with semi-transparent white/gray, apply Perlin noise for organic texture
- Wipe: on touch/mouse move, draw circles on fog canvas with `globalCompositeOperation: 'destination-out'`
- Wipe radius: 30-40px, with soft edge (radial gradient eraser, not hard circle)
- Fog creep-back: every 2-3 seconds, slightly re-fill wiped areas with low-opacity fog
- Fog drift: animate the noise offset slowly so fog appears to swirl
- Completion: when >70% wiped, fade the fog layer out entirely (reward moment)
- Content underneath: centered text with warm background, faint bokeh or candlelight

**Emotional tone:** Playful, romantic, intimate
**Best for block types:** Love Letter, Inside Joke, Memory, Scratch Reveal variant

### 4. Accordion Timeline Reveal

**Concept:** The surprise unfolds as a vertical timeline — each entry is collapsed and expands when tapped (accordion). Each entry has a date/label on the spine and the full memory/message inside. The timeline line draws as you progress (SVG stroke animation). Small icons/emoji mark each node.

**Why it works for Closr:** Perfect for "Story of Us" and milestone surprises. The accordion mechanic means only one memory is in focus at a time — each one gets its moment. The drawing timeline line creates visual continuity and a sense of progression.

**Technical approach:**
- Vertical flex layout with a thin line (`2px border-left` or SVG `<line>`)
- Each node: a circle on the line + collapsed card (date/title visible, content hidden)
- Expand: `max-height` transition + `opacity` on tap (or IntersectionObserver for auto-expand on scroll)
- Timeline draw: SVG `stroke-dashoffset` animation keyed to scroll position
- Node markers: small circles that fill with color as you reach them (empty → filled)
- Content inside each card: text + optional small image
- Stagger: each card expand triggers a subtle `scale(1.01)` bounce

**Emotional tone:** Nostalgic, structured, celebratory
**Best for block types:** Story of Us, Anniversary, Milestone, Appreciation Report

### 5. Slot Machine / Reel Spin Reveal

**Concept:** Content is hidden behind spinning reels — like a slot machine. Viewer taps to stop each reel, locking in a word/phrase that builds the message. "Things I love about you: [SPIN] your laugh [SPIN] your stubborn streak [SPIN] the way you steal my hoodies."

**Why it works for Closr:** Turns a compliment list into a game. The anticipation of each spin creates dopamine hits. Reframes sweet messages as rewards rather than passive text. The slot machine metaphor (jackpot = you) is playful and flattering.

**Technical approach:**
- CSS `overflow: hidden` containers with inner strips that `translateY` to simulate spinning
- Each reel: a tall div with multiple options stacked, animated with `cubic-bezier(0.15, 0.8, 0.3, 1)` for deceleration
- Tap to stop: removes spin animation, transitions to final position
- Lock-in effect: brief `scale(1.05)` + glow on winning item
- 3-5 reels per block
- After all reels locked: celebratory moment — confetti, pulse, or golden frame
- Sound: mechanical click-click-click (Web Audio oscillator, short burst at ~8Hz)

**Emotional tone:** Fun, playful, celebratory, gamified
**Best for block types:** Appreciation, Hype Up, Inside Joke, Challenge

### 6. Peel-Away Sticker Reveal

**Concept:** Content is hidden under decorative stickers (hearts, stars, custom emoji). Viewer peels each one away with a swipe gesture — the sticker lifts from one corner with a 3D peel effect, revealing text/image underneath.

**Why it works for Closr:** Stickers are the native visual language of messaging apps. Turning them into interactive reveal elements feels familiar yet magical. The peel gesture is satisfying. Each reveal is a micro-surprise within the surprise.

**Technical approach:**
- Each sticker: a `div` with `transform-origin` at one corner
- Peel animation: `rotateY(-180deg)` + `translateZ(10px)` on swipe, with curved shadow underneath
- Under-sticker content: text or small image, fades in as sticker lifts past 90°
- Sticker visuals: CSS-drawn hearts/stars or small inline SVGs
- Arrangement: scattered naturally (slight random rotation, varying sizes)
- After all peeled: stickers drift away, full message assembles

**Emotional tone:** Playful, cute, tactile, surprising
**Best for block types:** Appreciation, Gift Reveal, Memory, Love Letter
