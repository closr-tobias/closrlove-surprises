# Closr Block Research — March 12, 2026
## Focus: Particle Systems & Canvas Animations for Emotional Scenes

Previous research: March 8 covered typography/text animation treatments. This session explores **particle-based and canvas-driven visual effects** — ambient, physics-based, and interactive systems that create atmosphere and wonder.

Existing blocks reviewed: 16 blocks including `celebration-fireworks` (already uses particles), `future-constellation` (star-like), `countdown-heartbeat`. This research avoids those exact patterns and focuses on NEW particle/canvas approaches.

---

### 1. Firefly / Bioluminescence Field

**Concept:** A dark scene filled with softly glowing particles that drift organically, like fireflies in a summer field or bioluminescent plankton. The viewer's touch/drag creates gentle disturbances — particles scatter and slowly reform. Text content floats among the particles.

**Why it works for Closr:** Creates a dreamy, intimate nighttime atmosphere. The interactivity ("your touch moves the light") is a metaphor for connection. Perfect for late-night messages or comfort blocks.

**Technical approach:**
- Canvas 2D with 80-150 particles
- Each particle: `x, y, vx, vy, radius (2-6px), opacity (0.3-0.9), hue (warm yellow-green range)`
- Movement: Perlin noise-driven drift (not random — creates organic flow patterns)
- Glow: `ctx.shadowBlur = 15; ctx.shadowColor = particle.color`
- Touch interaction: particles within 80px radius get a gentle repulsion force, then spring back
- Pulse: each particle's opacity oscillates sinusoidally at slightly different frequencies (0.5-2Hz)
- Performance: requestAnimationFrame, offscreen canvas for glow compositing
- Text: HTML overlay with `pointer-events: none`, semi-transparent dark panel behind text

**Emotional tone:** Intimate, dreamy, peaceful
**Best for block types:** Comfort, Love Letter, Bedtime, Memory Replay

### 2. Ink Bloom / Watercolor Diffusion

**Concept:** Content reveals itself through ink-drop animations on canvas — each section of the surprise blooms outward like ink dropped in water, with organic tendrils and color bleeding. As the viewer scrolls/taps, new ink drops appear, revealing the next piece of content.

**Why it works for Closr:** The organic, imperfect nature of watercolor feels handmade and personal. Each "bloom" is a moment of revelation. The spreading ink metaphor — something small becoming something beautiful — mirrors how small relationship moments become meaningful memories.

**Technical approach:**
- Canvas 2D with fluid simulation (simplified Navier-Stokes or cellular automata)
- Alternatively: pre-rendered WebP sprite sheets for ink animations (much lighter, ~200KB per bloom)
- Lighter approach: radial gradient circles that expand with Gaussian blur + multiply blend mode
- Color palette per theme: rose/burgundy for romantic, indigo/teal for dreamy, warm amber for comfort
- Reveal mechanic: content divs start `opacity: 0`, ink bloom plays at their position, content fades in as bloom reaches 80% expansion
- CSS `mix-blend-mode: multiply` on canvas over a cream/paper background
- Each bloom: 1.5-2.5s duration, slight randomization in spread pattern

**Emotional tone:** Artistic, organic, surprising
**Best for block types:** Story, Appreciation Report, Memory Replay, Love Letter

### 3. Constellation Drawing (Connect-the-Dots)

**Concept:** NOT the existing `future-constellation` block (which shows a static starfield). This is an **interactive drawing** experience — dots appear one by one, and as the viewer connects them (tap to tap, or auto-drawing with a glowing line), a meaningful shape emerges: a heart, their initials intertwined, a map outline of meaningful places, or an abstract shape.

**Why it works for Closr:** Active participation makes the reveal feel earned. The viewer literally draws their love story. The anticipation of "what shape is this?" creates suspense. Works beautifully as an opener block before the main content.

**Technical approach:**
- Canvas 2D, points defined as coordinates along the target shape's outline
- Points appear with a soft pulse animation (scale 0 → 1 with overshoot easing)
- Auto-connect mode: line draws between points at 400ms intervals with a glowing trail
- Interactive mode: tap a point → line animates to next logical point
- Line rendering: `ctx.lineWidth = 2`, glow via double-render (thick blur line underneath, thin crisp line on top)
- Completion: shape fills with a soft gradient wash, text content appears inside/around
- Shape generation: AI outputs coordinate arrays as part of the content slot

**Emotional tone:** Playful, anticipatory, rewarding
**Best for block types:** Story, Inside Joke, Countdown, Celebration

### 4. Gravity Well / Magnetic Poetry

**Concept:** Words and short phrases float freely in zero-gravity space. The viewer can tap/hold anywhere to create a "gravity well" that attracts nearby words. Drag words together to form the message. Or: auto-mode where words gradually drift toward center, assembling the message poetically over time.

**Why it works for Closr:** The physics-based interaction is inherently playful and satisfying. Words literally being "drawn together" mirrors the couple being drawn together. There's delight in watching chaos become meaning.

**Technical approach:**
- Each word is a DOM element with position tracked in a physics simulation
- Simple Verlet integration for position updates
- Gravity well: `F = G * m / r²` toward touch point, with drag damping
- Words have slight random initial velocities and slow rotation
- Final state: words arranged in reading order, subtle breathing animation
- Spring constraints snap words to final grid positions once they're "close enough"
- Visual: words in different sizes/opacities based on emotional weight (AI-assigned)

**Emotional tone:** Playful, interactive, wonder
**Best for block types:** Love Letter, Appreciation Report, Inside Joke, Hype Up

### 5. Rain on Glass with Finger Drawing

**Concept:** Screen appears as a rain-covered window — realistic water droplets stream down, condensation fogs the glass. The viewer can "draw" on the fogged glass with their finger, revealing a message beneath. After a pause, the fog slowly creeps back in.

**Why it works for Closr:** Universally nostalgic — everyone has drawn on a foggy window. The tactile intimacy of finger-drawing maps perfectly to a touch screen. The impermanence (fog creeping back) creates urgency. Beautiful for rainy-day comfort messages.

**Technical approach:**
- Three canvas layers: background (blurred photo/gradient), fog (semi-transparent noise), rain (animated droplets)
- Rain drops: canvas particles with gravity + slight horizontal wind, leaving wet trails
- Fog clear on touch: circular mask on fog layer, radius 30-40px, with soft edge
- Fog regrowth: slowly increase opacity of cleared areas (0.5% per frame)
- Droplet physics: variable sizes (2-8px), speed proportional to size, merge on collision
- Pre-reveal option: AI message written as cleared areas that slowly appear through fog
- Performance: two offscreen canvases, composite to visible canvas

**Emotional tone:** Intimate, nostalgic, cozy
**Best for block types:** Comfort, Love Letter, Memory Replay

### 6. Paper Lantern Release

**Concept:** The scene starts dark. A single paper lantern appears at the bottom of the screen, glowing warmly. The viewer taps/holds to "release" it — it rises slowly, illuminating text as it passes. Then another lantern appears, and another. Each carries a word, phrase, or memory upward. Eventually the sky is filled with floating lanterns.

**Why it works for Closr:** The Tangled/Yi Peng lantern festival aesthetic is universally romantic. Each lantern is a discrete emotional unit — one memory, one compliment, one promise. The cumulative effect of a sky full of warm light is breathtaking.

**Technical approach:**
- Canvas 2D for lantern rendering, HTML overlay for readable text
- Lantern shape: rounded rectangle with flame flicker inside (oscillating orange-yellow gradient)
- Physics: gentle upward force + sinusoidal horizontal drift + slight rotation
- Light emission: radial gradient around each lantern, affects background darkness
- Release mechanic: tap-and-hold charges the lantern (grows brighter), release sends it up
- Text attachment: each lantern has a small text label drawn on canvas or as positioned HTML
- Background: gradient from deep navy (bottom) to dark purple (top), subtle star dots
- 10-20 lanterns total for Tier 2, 5-8 for Tier 1

**Emotional tone:** Romantic, ceremonial, breathtaking
**Best for block types:** Memory Replay, Promise, Appreciation Report, Future Vision, Celebration

### 7. Heartbeat Sync Visualizer

**Concept:** NOT the existing `countdown-heartbeat`. This is a rhythm visualizer — the viewer taps along with their pulse, and the visualization syncs to create a shared rhythm alongside a partner's pre-recorded pattern. Two hearts beating together across distance.

**Why it works for Closr:** "Our hearts beating together" made literal. Even simulated — two rhythm visualizers pulsing in sync — creates a visceral sense of connection across distance.

**Technical approach:**
- Two concentric ring visualizers on canvas
- Inner ring: "partner's heartbeat" — pre-set rhythm at ~72 BPM, warm rose color
- Outer ring: "your heartbeat" — responds to screen taps, cool blue color
- When rhythms align: rings merge color (purple), particles burst outward
- Optional soft heartbeat sound via Web Audio API, panned L/R
- Haptic feedback on each beat (navigator.vibrate if available)
- After 10-15 seconds of sync: text message fades in at center

**Emotional tone:** Intimate, visceral, connected
**Best for block types:** Comfort, Countdown, Love Letter (as opener)

## Performance Notes

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Firefly Field | 80-150 | Canvas 2D | Low | ✅ |
| Ink Bloom | N/A (fluid) | Canvas 2D / sprites | Medium | ✅ (use sprites) |
| Constellation Draw | 20-50 points | Canvas 2D | Low | ✅ |
| Gravity Well Words | 15-40 words | DOM + JS physics | Medium | ✅ (<30 words) |
| Rain on Glass | 50-200 drops | Canvas 2D (3 layers) | Medium-High | ⚠️ reduce on low-end |
| Paper Lanterns | 10-20 | Canvas 2D | Low-Medium | ✅ |
| Heartbeat Sync | 2 rings + ripples | Canvas 2D | Low | ✅ |

**General canvas tips for Closr blocks:**
- Always use `devicePixelRatio` for crisp Retina rendering
- Cap particle counts on older devices: `navigator.hardwareConcurrency < 4 ? halfCount : fullCount`
- Use `will-change: transform` on canvas element for GPU compositing
- Prefer `requestAnimationFrame` over `setInterval`
- Offscreen canvas for glow/blur effects
- Test on iPhone SE and budget Androids
